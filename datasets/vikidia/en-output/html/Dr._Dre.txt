Dr. Dre
Dr. Dre (real name: Andre Romelle Young) is an American rapper, beatmaker, producer, and entrepreneur. He was born on February 18, 1965, in Compton, California. Dr. Dre is one of the founding fathers of gangsta rap and has collaborated with several great rappers. He has produced albums by famous rappers, such as Eminem and 50 Cent.
Dr. Dre was a member of the founding gangsta rap group, N.W.A along with Ice Cube, MC Ren, Eazy-E, DJ Yella, and Arabian Prince. He released his first solo album, The Chronic in 1992. Dr. Dre created his own label, Aftermath Entertainment, in 1996. In 2006, with Jimmy Iovine, he created Beats Electronics, his brand of headphones, earphones, and speakers. In 2014, Apple bought the company for $3 billion.
Rappers like Nas and 50 Cent consider him the greatest hip-hop producer of all time.
