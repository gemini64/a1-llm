The US-Bangla Airlines is the largest private airline in Bangladesh and the second largest Bangladeshi airline after Biman Bangladesh Airlines.
US-Bangla (S2-AKL) ATR 72-600 (72-212A) MSN-1590

History
The airline started its operations on July 17, 2014 by operating domestic flights with the first two aircraft. It is a subsidiary of US-Bangla Group, a joint venture between the United States and Bangladesh.

Destinations
US-Bangla Airlines initially launched passenger services from two domestic destinations, Dhaka to Chittagong and Jessore. As of January 2020, US-Bangla Airlines is operating regular flights to the following destinations.

Accidents and incidents

On 12 March 2017, US-Bangla Airlines Flight 211 crashed while landing at Tribhuvan International Airport in Kathmandu, Nepal. There were a total of 71 passengers on board. Of these, 51 were killed and 20 were rescued alive.



On 26 September 2017, the US-Bangladesh BS-141 flight from Hazrat Shahjalal International Airport to Cox's Bazar made an emergency landing at Shah Amanat International Airport in Chittagong after failing to land at Cox's Bazar Airport due to an error. The US-Bangladesh Boeing 737-800 (Registration S2-AJA) is able to land safely without nose gear without rear gear, even if there is a risk of a fatal accident due to not opening the nose gear or front wheel. The plane was able to land safely with 11 children (infant) with 164 passengers and 6 crew. The flight was operated by Captain Zakaria.
