Theodore Robert Bundy (né Cowell; November 24, 1946 – January 24, 1989) was an American serial killer who kidnapped, raped, and murdered numerous young women and girls during the 1970s and possibly earlier. After more than a decade of denials, before his execution in 1989 he said he had done 30 homicides that he committed in seven states between 1974 and 1978. He died by the electric chair. He escaped jail a few times.
