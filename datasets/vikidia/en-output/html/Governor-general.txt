Governor-general is a role held by a person appointed by any head of state.  The term is most commonly used in the British Commonwealth, which is a group of countries of which the head of state is the Monarch of the United Kingdom, currently Charles III.   Different countries within the Commonwealth, use differing spellings of the term.  In Australia, the title is hyphenated, whereas in Canada, it is "Governor General".
