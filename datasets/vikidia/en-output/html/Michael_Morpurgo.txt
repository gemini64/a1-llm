Michael Morpurgo, the famous children's writer, was born in 1943 in St Albans, England. At eighteen years old, he entered the Sandhurst Military Academy, but then abandoned the army and married his wife Claire, and afterwards became a teacher. In 1982, he wrote his first book War Horse, which launched his writing career. It became a classic novel and the work was adapted for cinema by Steven Spielberg.
