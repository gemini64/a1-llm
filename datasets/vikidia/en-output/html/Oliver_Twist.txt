Oliver Twist, or The Parish Boy's Progress is a novel by Charles Dickens. It was published by Richard Bentley in 1838.
The book tells the story of a naive orphan boy who is led into a life of crime by a band of young pickpockets. Famous characters include: Mr. Brownlow, the Artful Dodger, Bill Sikes, Nancy, and Fagin. The novel has been the basis for several movies, stage plays, and the 1960 British musical, Oliver!
