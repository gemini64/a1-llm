Darvaza gas crater
The Darvaza gas crater, located in Darwaza, Turkmenistan, is a natural gas field that has been burning for a long time. It's also known as "Hell's Gate" due to the continuous fire that has been burning since 1971. The crater is situated in the Karakum Desert, and its diameter is 69 meters (226 ft), while its length is 30 meters (98 ft). Geologists set fire to the crater to stop the spread of methane gas.
Many tourists visit Darwaza town each year to witness the fire face. Since 2009, about 50,000 tourists have visited the place. The gas field is over 5350 square meters, and the surrounding area is famous for wild wilderness camping.
