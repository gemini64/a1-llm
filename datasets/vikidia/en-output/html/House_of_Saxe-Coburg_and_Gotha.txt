House of Saxe-Coburg and Gotha is a European royal house.
The House of Saxe-Coburg and Gotha was founded in 1826 by Ernest I, Duke of Saxe-Coburg and Gotha. It is a cadet branch of the Saxon House of Wettin. The current head is Andreas, Prince of Saxe-Coburg and Gotha.
In 1917, the First World War caused the British King George V to change the name from the "House of Saxe-Coburg and Gotha" to the House of Windsor.
