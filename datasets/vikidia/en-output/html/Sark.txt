Sercq (French: Sercq) is a small island in the southern part of the English Channel. It is an island in the Channel Islands and part of the Bailiwick of Guernsey. As such, the island is under British rule. The main industries of the island, which has a population of about 600, are tourism, crafts and trade. Its area is only 2 square miles (5.45 sq km). Feudalism has existed on this island for a long time. Recently this system has been abolished. Through this, the last feudalism of Europe also became extinct.
