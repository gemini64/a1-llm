The South Asian Association for Regional Cooperation (SAARC for short) is a regional organization in South Asia. Its member countries are Bangladesh, Pakistan, India, Sri Lanka, Maldives, Nepal, Bhutan and Afghanistan. China, Japan, the United States, South Korea, Iran, Myanmar, Mauritius and Australia are the six SAARC observer states. SAARC was established on December 6, 1975 by President Ziaur Rahman of Bangladesh. When the leaders of Bangladesh, India, Pakistan, Nepal, Bhutan, Maldives and Sri Lanka signed a royal charter aimed at promoting friendly relations and cooperation with the regional, economic, social and cultural development of South Asia and other developing countries. It is dedicated to emphasizing the collective self-reliance of economic, technological, social, cultural and development. The founding members of SAARC are Bangladesh, India, Pakistan, Sri Lanka, Nepal, Maldives, Bhutan and Afghanistan became members of SAARC in 2006. The summits of the states are usually scheduled on an annual basis and the meetings of the foreign secretaries are held every two years. Kathmandu, the capital of Nepal, is the headquarters of SAARC.

History
The first proposal to establish a framework for South Asian regional cooperation came from the thoughts of former President of Bangladesh Ziaur Rahman.
