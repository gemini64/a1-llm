François-Marie Arouet (21 November 1694 – 30 May 1778), known by his nom de plume M. de Voltaire was a French Enlightenment writer, philosopher (philosophe), satirist and historian. He was famous for his wit and criticism of Christianity (especially of the Roman Catholic Church) and of slavery. Voltaire was an advocate of freedom of speech, freedom of religion, and separation of church and state.
