Cortana on Windows 11

Cortana was a virtual assistant developed by Microsoft. Microsoft made Cortana and released it on April 2, 2014 at the Moscone Center in San Francisco. Cortana can do many things, like showing the weather and news. Cortana was released three years after Apple's Siri, which was released in 2011 on iOS. (*)

Development history
Microsoft showed Cortana on April 2 and April 4, 2014 at the Microsoft Build in San Francisco. Bill Gates, Steve Ballmer, and Joe Belfiore made the announcement. They said Cortana was virtual helper for the Windows Phone, starting with a video launch on April 6, 2014.
On July 15, 2015, Microsoft launched Cortana on Windows (Update version -1507), which was available to consumers on July 29, 2015. Cortana was released for Microsoft Band and Windows Mixed Reality on August 2, 2015 and for Android, IOS, iPhone and iPad in December 2015.

Discontinued
On June 2, 2023, Microsoft announced they will be no longer support Cortana standalone app on Windows 10 and Windows 11 and will be ended on late 2023. After Cortana ended support, Microsoft will announced Cortana will be replaced by Microsoft Copilot and Bing Chat AI on Windows 11. (*)  Microsoft discontinued Cortana standalone app for Windows 10 and Windows 11 on August 2023. Supported of Cortana in Microsoft Outlook and Microsoft 365 Mobile app was discontinued in Fall 2023. After discontinued, “Play My Emails” for Outlook mobile app will continue to supported. Finally after 1 years discontinued of standalone app, in June 2024, ''Play My Emails'' for Outlook mobile app was discontinued by worldwide, and now Cortana has not available in all worldwide.
