Bihać is a city located in the western part of Bosnia and Herzegovina, situated on the Una River, known for its natural beauty. Bihać is also a administrative centre of the Unsko-Sanski Canton and has historically been an important center for trade and culture. The city is near the border with Croatia.
Bihać is known for its rich history, which dates back to the Roman period, but it is most notable during the medieval era and Ottoman rule. The city was an important military and administrative center at various times, and today it is recognized for its cultural monuments and architecture.
Today, Bihać is known for its natural beauty and tourist attractions, with Una National Park being a best example. The park is home to numerous rivers, lakes, and waterfalls, and it is particularly popular among nature lovers, hikers, rafters, and outdoor enthusiasts.
