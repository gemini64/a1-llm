Immanuel Kant (born Emanuel Kant; 22 April 1724 – 12 February 1804) was a German philosopher and one of the central Enlightenment thinkers.
Immanuel Kant was born on 22 April 1724 in Königsberg, East Prussia. He studied philosophy at the University of Königsberg, he later became a professor of philosophy. He called his system "transcendental idealism". Kant's writing about epistemology, metaphysics, ethics, and aesthetics have made him one of the most influential figures in the history of philosophy.
Immanuel Kant died on 12 February 1804 in Königsberg.
