Colchester is a city in Essex, England. It is the oldest recorded city in Britain.
Colchester is first recorded in 77 AD by Roman writer Pliny the Elder when the city was called Camulodunum. A Roman Temple was built in Colchester, the Temple and much of the city was burned down by Queen Boudica during her revolt. In the 11th century, Colchester Castle was constructed by William the Conqueror.
Colchester is known for Colchester Zoo, among other landmarks.
The MP for Colchester is Pam Cox, a Labour Party politican.
