Samuel Hahnemann
Christian Friedrich Samuel Hahnemann (German: [ˈhaːnəman]; 10 April 1755 – 2 July 1843) was a famous German physician and the inventor of homoeopathic medicine.
Hahnemann introduced homeopathy treatment in 1805. In 1810, he published the Organon of Rational Healing Art (Organon der rationellen Heilkunde) in German, later known as the Organon of Medicine. He taught homoeopathy at the University of Leipzig from 29 September 1812 to 1821. Get success in speaking. He left Germany in June 1835 and settled in Paris, where he died in 1843.
