The Old Testament (OT) is the first division of the Christian biblical canon, based primarily on the 24 books of the Hebrew Bible, or Tanakh.
The Old Testament was originally written in old religious Hebrew and occassionally Aramaic but has been translated into many languages since.
The Eastern Orthodox and Oriental Orthodox bibles contain up to 49 books, the Catholic bibles contain 46 and the most common Protestant canons contain 39.
