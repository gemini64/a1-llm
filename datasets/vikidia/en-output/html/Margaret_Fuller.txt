Margaret Fuller
Margaret Fuller was an American writer and advocate of women's rights.
She lived from 1810 to 1850. She was the editor of Ralph Waldo Emerson's magazine about Transcendentalism.
She was a reporter for the New York Tribune newspaper.
Her most important work was the book Woman in the Nineteenth Century.
She may have married an Italian nobleman, the Marchese Ossoli. They had a son together.
They helped the revolution in Italy and were forced to leave Europe.
She and her family were drowned in a shipwreck in the Atlantic Ocean near New York.
