The Adriatic Sea
The Adriatic Sea (Italian: Mare Adriatico, Slovenian: Jadránsko mórje/Jadrán, Croatian and Bosnian: Jadransko more, Serbian: Јадранско море, Albanian: Deti Adriatik, Latin: Hadriaticum)  is a sea in Europe. It's part of the Mediterranean Sea and borders Slovenia, Croatia, Bosnia and Herzegovina, Montenegro, Albania and Italy. The Adriatic Sea is a touristic attraction, especially along the coast of Italy. Important cities at the Adriatic Sea are: Bari, Venetia, Trieste (Italy), Zadar, Dubrovnik (Croatia) and Durrës (Albania). The largest islands are Krk and Cres (both Croatian).
