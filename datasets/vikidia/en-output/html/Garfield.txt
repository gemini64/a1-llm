Garfield is the orange cat which has reached the hearts of many within the modern day. He is depicted as a lazy, fat and lasagne loving cat who is very against the thought of Mondays. Created in 1978 by Jim Davis, the comic highlighted the life of Garfield, his pal Odie and the oblivious owner, Jon Arbuckle. Garfield's daily life consists of eating lasagne, smart and sly comments, hating on Nermal and being a lazy fat cat. Due to his relatable lifestyle, he has appeared in many current forms of multimedia such as memes or GIFs, thus showing that Garfield has reached the epitome of a great comic strip.

The Success of Garfield has caused many spin offs to occur, including multiple TV shows like 'The Garfield Show' which spawned a new cult of following resulting in merchandise to be sold at an exponential rate. A television cartoon show, Garfield and Friends, aired for seven seasons from 1988 to 1994; this adaption also starred Lorenzo Music as the voice of Garfield.
Garfield has also reached a high success within the Cinema side of film industry resulting in many box office appearances. Garfield: The Movie was released in theaters on June 11, 2004. Its sequel, Garfield: A Tail of Two Kitties, was released on June 16, 2006. Three direct-to-video films were released, Garfield Gets Real on August 9, 2007, Garfield's Fun Fest on August 5, 2008, and Garfield's Pet Force on June 16, 2009. On May 24, 2016, it was announced that Alcon Entertainment will develop a new CG animated Garfield movie with John Cohen and Steven P. Wegner ready to produce.
