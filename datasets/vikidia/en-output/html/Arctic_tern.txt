The Arctic tern is a kind of bird in the Laridae family. Its scientific name is Sterna paradisaea. It is known for making the world's longest animal migration.

Description
A chick.
The Arctic tern measures between 33 and 36 cm. It has small legs and a forked tail.
It has white and grey plumage with a black spot on the top of its head. Legs and wings are dark red. It should not be confused with the common tern, which looks very similar but does not live in the same place.
The chicks are dark grey or brown with a white belly.
