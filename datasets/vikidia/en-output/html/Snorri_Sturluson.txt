Snorri Sturluson
Snorri Sturluson was a poet, writer, and leader from Iceland. He lived from about 1179 to 1241.
He is the most important person in Scandinavian writing in the Middle Ages.
He wrote a lively history of the kings of Norway, the Heimskringla.
He also wrote a book called the Younger Edda. It tells about Norse myth and poetry.
