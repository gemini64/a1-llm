Lymphoma is a type of cancer which affects a part of the Immune system called the Lymphatic system.  The Lymphatic system makes and carries special cells called Lymphocytes (White blood cells, also known as Leukocytes) around your body, which help it to fight illnesses and infections.
There are lots of different types of Lymphoma, which all impact the body in different ways; the two most commonly seen types are Hodgkin's Lymphoma and non-Hodgkin Lymphoma, both of which are usually treated with Chemotherapy.
