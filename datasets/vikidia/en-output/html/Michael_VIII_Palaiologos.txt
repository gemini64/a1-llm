Miniature of Michael VIII Palaiologos.
Michael VIII Palaiologos (1223-1282) was a Nicaean Emperor and a Byzantine emperor from 1259 until his death. In 1261, he reconquered the Byzantine capitale Constantinople from the Crusaders and was the founder of the Palaiologos dynasty.
He became emperor of the Nicaean Empire in 1259 after overthrowing the previous emperor, John IV Laskaris, who was aged of 11.
After his death, his son Andronikos II Palaiologos succeeds him.
