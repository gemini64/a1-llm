Confucius (孔子; pinyin: Kǒngzǐ; lit. 'Master Kong'; c. 551 – c. 479 BCE), born Kong Qiu (孔丘), was a Chinese philosopher of the Spring and Autumn period who is traditionally considered the paragon of Chinese sages, and the first teacher in China to advocate for mass education.
He was born in Zou, the state of Lu, a vassal state of the Zhou dynasty, he was born in what is now the city of Qufu in the Shandong province of eastern China. He died near the Si River in the state of Lu.
