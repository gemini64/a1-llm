Oscar Robertson is a retired basketball player nicknamed "Big O". He was born on November 24, 1938, in Charlotte, Tennessee. He grew up in Indianapolis, Indiana where he won two state championships in school. Robertson later attended the University of Cincinnati where he was the first Black American basketball player.
After college, he was drafted by the NBA team Cincinnati Royals in 1960. He later played for the Milwaukee Bucks where he led them to the NBA championship in 1971.
Robertson retired from basketball in 1974. He received his NBA Lifetime Achievement award in 2018.
