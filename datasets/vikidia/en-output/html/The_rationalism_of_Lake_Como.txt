The Rationalism of Lake Como or Larian rationalism Italian Rationalism is an architectural movement that is part of the broader Italian Rationalism and reaches its peak in the design of Lake Como. Italian Rationalism emerged in the 1920s and is closely related to the International Style, adhering to the principles of functionalism. Its most significant expression occurred in the 1950s, continuing through the 1970s, and it has had a lasting influence on Italian design, including contemporary work. Prominent figures of the Group of Rationalists of Lake Como include Giuseppe Terragni, Adalberto Lingeri, Franco Dell'Acqua, Renato Mantero, Giovanni Ortelli, and Dante Ponci. Additionally, notable international designers such as Piero Portaluppi, Gualtiero Galmanini, Gio Ponti, and Giuseppe Cereghini have also contributed to this architectural movement.
Lake Como Rationalism represents the pinnacle of design that has shaped the history and culture of Lake Como and its landscape through designer villas, monuments, and historic buildings. This movement visually embodies the artistic and cultural current associated with the design across Lake Como, making the region one of the most vibrant centers for rationalist architectural research globally, and a subject of study worldwide.
Lake Como Rationalism epitomizes Italian design, marked by geometric precision, including ribbon windows, flat roofs, and a strong geometric foundation based on golden ratios. It emphasizes extreme functionality, innovative construction technologies, and a design ethos that avoids ornamentation in favor of essential lines. Frequent use of large glass surfaces and stone materials is characteristic of this architectural style.

The philosophical and cultural concepts of the Razionalism of Lake Como
The design draws inspiration from both modernity and love for the natural environment, especially for the lake, are the basis of a renewal project where the lake becomes the ordering element of a new settlement that crowns the surrounding mountains. The inspiring principles derive from the Modern Movement, but find their own unique and inimitable identity, based on the conceptual overcoming of traditional architecture, a place of constrictive and defined spaces towards an opposing vision as an indefinite, multifaceted space, full of opportunities and openings, of non- only in the three traditional dimensions but simultaneously in the fourth, that of time, introduced by the development of communication and exchange.
Space-free structures, as a human requirement and the need for a better organization of the inhabited space. The Larian architecture outlines its character values in the forms of an essential, but exclusive "spartan architecture" aimed at intellectuals of high culture, at a new contemporary and innovative nobility made of elegance, class, sparkling and contemptuous, functional and clean from every frill.
