The Zika virus (ZIKV) is a virus spread by mosquitoes, it is a member of the virus family Flaviviridae.
Since the 1950s, it has been known to occur in the narrow equatorial belt from Africa to Asia. From 2007 to 2016, the virus spread eastward, across the Pacific Ocean to the Americas, leading to the 2015â€“2016 Zika virus epidemic.
According to the CDC in May 2024, there is no vaccine to prevent or medicine to treat the Zika disease. (*)

2016 outbreak
In 2016, a number of pregnant women were infected with Zika fever and their babies were born with unusually small heads.
