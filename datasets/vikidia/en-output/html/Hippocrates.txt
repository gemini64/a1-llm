Hippocrates of Kos (c. 460 – c. 370 BC), also known as Hippocrates II was a Greek doctor and philosopher, he is considered one of the most important figures in the history of medicine and is known as the "Father of Medicine".

Biography
Many historians agree that Hippocrates was born around 640 BC on the island of Kos in modern day Greece. However, other biographical information is considered likely to be untrue.
