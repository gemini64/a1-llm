Sendai after the earthquake
On 11 March 2011, at 14:46 local time (05:46 UTC), a very big earthquake happened in the Tōhoku region, Japan. It lasted around six minutes and caused a big tsunami. It is sometimes known in Japan as the "Great East Japan Earthquake" (東日本大震災, Higashi nihon daishinsai), among other names. This disaster is known as "3.11". The disaster is often called 3.11, which is read as "san ten ichi-ichi" in Japanese. (*)  (*)  (*)
It was the biggest earthquake ever happened in Japan, and the fourth most biggest earthquake in the world since modern seismography began in 1900. (*)  (*)  (*)  The earthquake triggered a big tsunami waves that may have reached heights of up to 40.5 meters (133 ft). (*)  Peoples said that 19,759 people died, 6,242 injured and 2,553 others missing. The tsunami also caused the Fukushima Daiichi nuclear disaster. (*)  (*)
